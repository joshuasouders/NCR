National Capital Region Urban Area Security Initiative: An Investment Overview


NCR Urban Area Security Initiative Awards FY03-FY14

Since FY03, FEMA has awarded the National Capital Region (NCR) more than $665 million in Urban Area Security Initiative (UASI) grant funds, making it the third largest UASI region in the nation (behind New York and Los Angeles). Maryland Specific NCR UASI Awards

In theory, the NCR UASI is designed to support emergency preparedness in each jurisdiction in the region equally: grant funds are awarded to individual jurisdictions, but each jurisdiction manages its project on behalf of the entire region.

In reality, many NCR UASI-funded projects are designed to benefit a single jurisdiction or state. On average, Maryland state agencies and localities have received approximately $9.8 million each year from the NCR UASI since FY08.

The District and Virginia Specific NCR UASI Awards

The District and Virginia also receive a share of the NCR UASI that benefits their jurisdictions specifically. The chart below shows their shares of the NCR UASI since FY08 compared to Maryland. Maryland's average annual share of the NCR UASI compares favorably to Virginia's average annual share ($9.9 million) and exceeds the District's average annual share ($6.2 million).

The remaining portion of each annual award is called the "regional" award. These are funds that support programs or projects that are implemented in each NCR jurisdiction and improve emergency preparedness throughout the region equally.

Maryland Specific NCR UASI Awards: Personnel

How do Maryland state and local agencies spend their NCR UASI funds? There are 5 core categories of expenditures. The first is Personnel. This may include training and exercise officers, emergency planners and intelligence analysts.

Maryland Specific NCR UASI Awards: Interoperability/Data Sharing

The second category is Interoperability and Data Sharing Equipment. This may include portable radios, radio system infrastructure, CAD-RMS system upgrades, WebEOC upgrades or other communication tools.

Maryland Specific NCR UASI Awards: Response Equipment

The third category is Emergency Response Equipment. This may include personal protective equipment (PPE) and other response equipment for police and fire/EMS departments.

Maryland Specific NCR UASI Awards: Facility Upgrades

The fourth category is Facility Upgrades. This may include audio-visual or physical upgrades to Emergency Operation Centers in the region and State.

Maryland Specific NCR UASI Awards: Maryland Emergency Response System

The fifth and final category (in addition to other/miscellaneous allowable expenses) is for the Maryland Emergency Response System (ERS) starting in FY12. This may include personnel, equipment and training and exercise services that are shared between Montgomery and Prince George's Counties.

NCR UASI Regional Projects: Radio Cache

In addition to the specific NCR UASI awards that Maryland receives, it also participates in and benefits from several regional projects that are shared equally among Maryland, Virginia and the District.

The first is the NCR Radio Cache, a deployable cache of portable radios and equipment that enhance the region's ability to respond and communicate during an emergency event. Since FY03, the region has invested more than $10.4 million in operating and maintaining the radio cache program.

NCR UASI Regional Projects: NCRNet

The NCRNet connects the region's individual government-run Intranet systems and enables cross-jurisdictional communication when normal Internet service is down or interrupted. Since FY04, the region has invested more than $30 million in operating and maintaining the NCRNet.

NCR UASI Regional Projects: Patient Tracking

Patient tracking equipment allows the region's medical first responders to tag and scan victims in a mass casualty incident, maintain a record of receiving facilities, and assist in family reunification. Since FY04, the region has invested more than $13 million to develop and maintain a patient tracking system.

NCR UASI Regional Projects: CAD-2-CAD

The CAD-2-CAD program integrates fire dispatch systems, allows for automated information sharing, and helps departments decrease response times. It currently operates only in Northern Virginia but is partially expanding to the District and Maryland jurisdictions. Since FY07, the region has invested approximately $6 million to develop and maintain the CAD-2-CAD program.

NCR UASI Regional Projects: Video Sharing

The video sharing program is currently managed by Maryland's fusion center. It provides participating agencies with video sharing hardware and technical support to allow them to share real-time video feeds with response partners during an emergency event. Since FY08, the region has invested approximately $6.4 million to develop and maintain the video sharing program.

NCR UASI Regional Projects: License Plate Readers

The license plate reader (LPR) program has purchased a mix of mobile and fixed license plate readers for the region's law enforcement jurisdictions (including fixed cameras on I-95 in Prince George's County). Since FY08, the region has invested more than $11 million to purchase and maintain license plate reader equipment.

NCR UASI Regional Projects: LINX

The Law Enforcement Information Exchange (LINX) program connects the region's RMS databases and allows law enforcement investigators to conduct cross-jurisdictional searches of criminal records. Since FY08, the region has invested more than $11 million to develop and maintain the LINX program.

NCR UASI Regional Projects: AFIS

The Automated Fingerprint Identification System (AFIS) program provides the regional law enforcement agencies with scanning hardware and technical support to quickly read suspect finger prints and match against criminal records. Since FY08, the region has invested approximately $22 million to develop and maintain the AFIS program.

NCR UASI Regional Projects: RITIS

The Regional Integrated Traffic Incident System (RITIS) is an online traffic map that displays real-time traffic incident data and analytics. Since FY09, the region has invested approximately $5.5 million to develop and maintain RITIS.

NCR UASI Regional Projects: Text Alert System

This program allows local jurisdictions to push out emergency incident information to residents via text and email messages. Since FY08, the region has invested more than $7 million to develop and maintain the text alert system.

NCR UASI Regional Projects: Mass Care

The region awards UASI funds to the Red Cross to purchase and maintain emergency shelter supplies (cots, blankets, pet shelters, meals, etc.). Since FY04, the region has invested more than $11 million in the Red Cross for mass care and emergency shelter services.

NCR UASI Regional Projects: ESSENCE

The region awards UASI funds to the Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Lab to develop and maintain the NCR ESSENCE (Electronic Surveillance System for the Early Notification of Community-based Epidemics) program. ESSENCE assists public health departments in detecting biological hazards, communicable diseases, and other public health threats. Since FY04, the region has invested approximately $7 million to develop and maintain the NCR ESSENCE.
